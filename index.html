01 · Digital diagnostic

The site is beautiful. The funnel isn't.

Dubbu.dk runs on Shopify with a theme that visually matches the premium brand you're building. The audit shows operational friction that's leaving money on the table every day.

Value proposition

Clear, but passive

"Copenhagen's first and only K-beauty store" is a winning position. The homepage doesn't turn that differentiator into action: no skin-type quiz, no recommender, no urgency. The curious visitor lands — then leaves for Google.

Customer journey

3 clicks to cart

Solid standard Shopify flow. Free shipping at 499 DKK lifts the average ticket. Same-day click & collect is a real edge over Notino and Care to Beauty. These benefits need to surface on the product page, not just in the header.

Customer service

Email only

The only visible channel is kontakt@dubbu.dk. No web chat, no WhatsApp, no AI agent. A customer with a routine question at 10pm closes the tab.

Why Trustpilot matters more in Denmark

Denmark is one of the highest per-capita Trustpilot markets in the world — the platform is Danish, and local consumers check it before buying far more than in Spain or the US. Low ranking + few reviews = the average shopper stays with Matas and Notino, who have thousands. The product isn't what competes here: social trust is. And social trust is built systematically, not by hand.

03 · Instagram & content

The handle is already the first signal.

Your account is @dubbu.926. It's the fallback handle Instagram suggests when the main one is taken — and on a premium brand, that's a label that says "we improvised." It's a detail, but the detail matters when you sell quiet elegance.

Observable fact

Visual content, not commercial

The feed is aesthetically aligned with the brand: beige tones, Korean packaging, Seoul-Copenhagen vibe. But it isn't wired to conversion: few tagged products, few calls to action, little UGC, no step-by-step routine Reels — the format that's converting in K-beauty globally.

Di Barret estimate

Hidden cost of content

Producing a decent Reel costs Dubbu about 2-3 human hours today (photo, edit, copy, scheduling). Publishing 3 pieces/week = 6-9 hours/week = ~30 hours/month. At a loaded internal rate (~290 DKK/h), that's ~8,700 DKK/month in hours alone. With no guaranteed conversion.

Dubbu's Instagram content is visual, but it doesn't reduce purchase friction. An automated system doesn't just improve the aesthetic — it funnels traffic into closed loops and measures which post actually generates carts.

Leak #1 · After-hours support
~12k DKK/month

Fact: The online store runs 24/7. Human support doesn't.
Cost: ~15-20% of relevant visits happen outside business hours. Without a reply, conversion roughly halves. With an estimated 350 DKK average ticket, that's 10-14k DKK/month in orders walking to a competitor.

Leak #2 · Trustpilot underused
~18k DKK/month

Fact: 10 reviews in 17 months. The standard for your size is 40-60 per year.
Cost: Without volume, your Meta/Google ads carry +25-35% inflated CAC (lower CTR, lower quality score). On a reasonable performance spend: 15-20k DKK/month you're paying on top.

Leak #3 · Manual content
~9k DKK/month

Fact: Producing Reels/posts today costs you ~30 human hours/month.
Cost: At a loaded rate of ~290 DKK/h in the Danish market, that's 8,700 DKK/month in hours — plus the opportunity cost of the team not selling in store or running logistics.

Leak #4 · Abandoned carts
~22k DKK/month

Fact: Beauty has the highest cart abandonment in e-commerce (~82%).
Cost: Without a designed recovery sequence, you recover half of what's recoverable. The gap between Shopify default and a well-built sequence is 15-25k DKK/month in rescued orders.

Leak #5 · Manual curation
~7k DKK/month

Fact: 641 SKUs and 90 brands. Hand-recommending "what to take home today" doesn't scale.
Cost: Every customer who leaves without buying because they didn't know what to pick is a lost AOV. A basic AI recommender (skin quiz + matching) lifts conversion 8-15%. At your volume: 5-9k DKK/month.

Leak #6 · Invisible repeat purchase
~14k DKK/month

Fact: In skincare, reorder cycles are 45-90 days. Without an automated replenishment email, you depend on customers remembering you.
Cost: A replenishment sequence per brand/product recaptures 10-18% of customers in the right window. Conservatively: 10-16k DKK/month in LTV.

Estimated total

Combined leak ≈ 80,000 DKK/month · ~960,000 DKK/year

These numbers are estimates built from public beauty-sector benchmarks + Danish operating costs — not your accounting figures. The point is to size the problem. Even if reality were half that, we're talking about half a million DKK a year in capturable value. That's what's on the table.

Current problem
